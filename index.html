About Me

I'm a current undergraduate student at the University of Massachusetts Lowell studying in Computer Science, with a minor in Robotics and Math.

My main focus is aimed towards Robotics. Before I was introduced to this field, I was debating between choosing Computer Science, or Engineering. I chose Computer Science and happy with the decision, but wished there was a field that was more physical and hand-on. A year into college, I realized I need to choose a specific field to specialize in. When I came across Robotics, I came to a realization that code could not only live in the digital realm but also control physical processes. Now I enjoy spending time building and learning, not just software, but the hardware perspective of robotics and computers. I participate in multiple projects, from a Mobile Robot for Impaired People to a Drone Swarm. I've also created small-to-medium web applications for robots and software I've developed.

I've recently came across the field of automation, which gave me a different prespective about controls, reliability, safety and UI. I'm currently gaining internship experience into controls, automation, PLCs, HMIs, and more!

In my spare time, I enjoy music engineering, video editing, and playing sports.
